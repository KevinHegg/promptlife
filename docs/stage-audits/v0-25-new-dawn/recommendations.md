# New Dawn Recommendations v0.25

Date: 2026-06-07

## Top Five Content Problems

1. Model Literate Synthesis does not explicitly connect the full mechanics chain in the prose or visual: tokens, token IDs, embeddings, vectors/tensors, hidden states, logits, softmax, sampling, autoregression, context, RAG, grounding, hallucination, costs, benefits, and accountability.
2. Better AI Is a Choice is accurate but list-heavy. Learners need a cleaner mental structure for levers: technical, data/provenance, institutional/governance, labor, sustainability, and evaluation.
3. Human-Centered AI risks sounding slogan-like unless learners get one concrete scenario about accountability, procurement, or high-stakes review.
4. Benefits Worth Taking Seriously should preserve evidence tiers but make the tier distinction more actionable.
5. New Dawn should more clearly close Midnight Ledger: costs and risks are real, but human choices shape design, deployment, review, and governance.

## Top Five Visual/UI Problems

1. Model Literate Synthesis most needs a stronger coded visual. The current map/compass/lantern scene is calm but too sparse for a capstone.
2. Better AI Is a Choice needs a clearer lever/control-panel or decision-path visual; the forked path is good but too binary.
3. Human-Centered AI needs a visual that shows people, institutions, accountability, and affected communities, not only an abstract center.
4. Benefits needs evidence-tier buckets in the visual or callouts so demonstrated, plausible, and speculative claims are not just prose.
5. Effective Prompting needs a true prompt-recipe/tray visual that visibly organizes task, context, constraints, examples, evidence, uncertainty, format, and review.

## Top Five Source-Review Problems

1. Benefits claims still need task-specific evidence before stronger statements about education, research, accessibility, or scientific workflow support.
2. Better AI mitigation claims need careful source boundaries, especially for energy/water reduction, lower-carbon electricity, data center siting, model distillation, and hardware utilization.
3. Labor transition planning and deskilling need stronger labor and education evidence before expansion.
4. Data provenance, consent, licensing, creator compensation, and opt-out claims should remain jurisdiction-aware and cautious.
5. Human-centered dignity sources are strong internally, but learner-facing copy should not become a religious-source lesson unless a future curriculum pass intentionally chooses that route.
