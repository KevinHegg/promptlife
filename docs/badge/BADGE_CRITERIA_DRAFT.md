# Prompt Life: Model Literate Badge Criteria Draft

Version: v0.27.12

Status: under construction. Criteria are not final.

## Badge Purpose

Prompt Life: Model Literate is intended to recognize practical model literacy: explaining what LLMs are, how a prompt becomes a response, what the model is not doing, and how humans should verify, review, and decide responsibly.

## Badge Status

Prompt Life: Model Literate is not yet issued. The Journey, checkpoint bank, and evidence model are being tested before any badge is issued.

Human review status: pending.

Final badge visual: pending final asset review. The app currently uses a placeholder-style badge panel on the Badge page.

## Draft Evidence Model

Practice evidence saved on the learner device may include:

- Journey learning cards completed out of 39.
- Stages completed out of 8.
- Checkpoint questions available across the full bank.
- Play challenges completed or reviewed out of 5.
- Prompt Run progress.
- Reflection notes.
- Last saved Play activity when available.

Checkpoint mastery tracking is being refined, so the Badge page does not yet claim a mastered-question count.

## Draft Criteria

- Complete the 39-card Journey.
- Demonstrate checkpoint mastery across the full active question bank.
- Complete or meaningfully review the five Play practice challenges.
- Show synthesis evidence across model basics, training and inference, prompt path, context and grounding, probability literacy, wider landscape, risk and myth literacy, and human-centered use.
- Receive human review before any badge is issued.

## Learning Objective Categories

1. Model basics.
2. Training and inference.
3. Prompt path.
4. Context and grounding.
5. Probability literacy.
6. Wider landscape.
7. Risk and myth literacy.
8. Human-centered use.

Each category can report not started, in progress, evidence collected, and pending human review states.

## Human Review Plan

Before public issuance, the badge needs human review of checkpoint validity, objective coverage, evidence thresholds, accessibility, mobile usability, and whether the evidence model measures model literacy rather than simple completion.

## Possible External Issuer Notes

If the badge later maps to a school, employer, or external microcredential issuer, the issuer should receive:

- Final criteria and evidence thresholds.
- A reviewed checkpoint bank and item-analysis summary.
- Accessibility and mobile QA notes.
- Clear language that the badge recognizes model literacy, not professional AI engineering certification.
