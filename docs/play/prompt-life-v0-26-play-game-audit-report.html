Recommended Final Slate

Prompt Run

Objective: Trace one prompt through tokenization, embeddings, layers, logits, softmax, sampling, append, and repeat.

Player action: Label, connect, choose, append, and order the inference loop.

Why it belongs: It is the capstone for the day-in-the-life prompt story and the only current Play activity with full-loop coverage.

Time: 6-8 min after revision | Play insight: yes

Context Stack

Objective: Understand the context window as temporary visible input, not permanent memory.

Player action: Push cards into a limited context window and watch older cards fall out.

Why it belongs: It is the clearest small game and directly supports context, RAG, grounding, and hallucination literacy.

Time: 3 min | Play insight: yes

Attention Match

Objective: Understand attention as weighted relevance between token positions, not human attention or awareness.

Player action: Connect a target token to the source token or phrase it most depends on.

Why it belongs: Attention is a core mystery-reducing concept, but it needs a clearer diagnostic mechanic than the current single arc.

Time: 3-4 min | Play insight: yes

Probability Picker

Objective: Understand logits, softmax, sampling, uncertainty, and why generation chooses one next token from probabilities.

Player action: Compare candidate next tokens, adjust a simple temperature control, and choose from probability-shaped options.

Why it belongs: Decision Room concepts currently need a stronger standalone practice activity.

Time: 3-4 min | Play insight: yes

Glossary Dojo

Objective: Build durable vocabulary for model literacy through definitions, relationships, and common mix-ups.

Player action: Answer 12 calm glossary questions with four choices and no scoring pressure.

Why it belongs: A vocabulary-rich app needs a replayable practice engine, and Dojo is already mature.

Time: 4 min | Play insight: no
