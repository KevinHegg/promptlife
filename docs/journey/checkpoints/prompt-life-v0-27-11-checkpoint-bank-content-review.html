5PretrainingExplain pretraining as broad durable pattern learning from many next-token targets, not perfect recall, live browsing, or a searchable archive.

7Fine-TuningDistinguish fine-tuning or adapter training as durable post-pretraining adaptation from prompt, RAG, and sampling-time steering.
8AlignmentReason about alignment as behavior shaping through training, prompts, policies, filters, evaluation, and deployment controls without treating it as conscience or truth guarantee.
9InferenceExplain inference as a forward pass using fixed weights and temporary context-shaped states, not durable learning.
10Prompt vs ResponseSeparate given prompt/context tokens from generated response tokens and explain that generated tokens are appended into the next inference context.

12Token IDsExplain token IDs as lookup keys from token chunks to embedding rows, not meanings, memories, or source retrieval by themselves.
13EmbeddingsDistinguish an embedding as a learned starting vector retrieved from a durable embedding table from a temporary hidden state shaped by context.
14VectorsExplain vectors as numerical representations with distributed features and distinguish vectors from scalar IDs, tensors, and human-readable labels.
15TensorsExplain tensors as shaped numerical blocks that organize many token vectors and features for layer operations.
16AttentionExplain attention as temporary weighted relevance between token positions that shapes hidden states, not human awareness or final token choice by itself.
17MLPExplain the MLP as per-token feature reshaping inside each layer, distinct from attention mixing across token positions.
18LayersExplain transformer layers as repeated attention-plus-MLP transformations that refine temporary hidden states without implying human thought steps.
19Hidden StatesDistinguish hidden states as temporary context-shaped internal vectors created during inference from embeddings, weights, visible text, and memory.
20LogitsExplain logits as temporary raw next-token scores produced before softmax, not probabilities, truth scores, or complete answers.
21SoftmaxExplain softmax as converting logits into a next-token probability distribution, not choosing the token or proving truth.
22SamplingExplain sampling as choosing one next token from probabilities during inference, after softmax and before append/repeat.
23AutoregressionExplain autoregression as next token, append to context, run again, while weights usually remain unchanged and context grows.
24Context WindowExplain the context window as temporary visible input space for the current run, not permanent memory, and show how autoregression and RAG compete for that space.
25RAG and RetrievalExplain RAG as a retrieval system that places outside evidence into current context during inference, not training, omniscience, or permanent memory.
26GroundingExplain grounding as connecting generated claims to available evidence while recognizing that citations or retrieval do not guarantee truth.
27HallucinationsExplain hallucination as fluent generated output that lacks adequate support, not intentional lying, consciousness, or proof of malice.

29Diffusion vs AutoregressionDistinguish autoregressive text generation from diffusion-style denoising and show that generative AI methods do not all work like ChatGPT.
30Multimodal AIExplain multimodal AI as systems that represent or process more than one media type together without implying human-like perception.
31The Perfect StormExplain modern LLM capability as convergence among data, compute, storage, methods, labor, and incentives, not one magic breakthrough.
32Collective Intelligence, ExtractedExplain that much model usefulness comes from patterns in human-created data and labor, raising questions of provenance, consent, and compensation.
33Costs We Must CountIdentify costs across training, inference, infrastructure, data, labor, privacy, and institutional deployment rather than treating digital systems as cost-free.
34Risk vs MythSeparate practical institutional risks from myths, focusing on privacy, bias, unsupported claims, overreliance, governance, and non-conscious models.
35Benefits Worth Taking SeriouslyName realistic AI benefits such as drafting, access, translation, summarization, and search while keeping limits, costs, and human review visible.
36Human-Centered AIExplain human-centered AI as keeping human judgment, accountability, oversight, appeal, and context at the center of AI-assisted decisions.
